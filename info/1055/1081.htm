Neuromorphic computing

APPT

(Chaired by Songwen Pei (swpei@usst.edu.cn), Elena Goi, University of Shanghai for Science and Technology, China)

In the big data era, artificial intelligence (AI) and in particular machine learning (ML) have an enormous impact on every sector of our lives. The latest breakthroughs in ML have been fueled by the processing power and parallelism of modern graphics processing units (GPUs). However, high-end accelerators running increasingly complex neural networks are hungry for power and bandwidth, require substantial processing times and bulky form factors. Analog ML hardware platforms promise to be faster and more energy efficient than their digital counterparts and wave physics, as found in acoustics and optics, is a natural candidate for building analog processors.

This targeted workshop will bring together researchers from computer science, electronic engineering, optics and photonics to consider analog computing platforms, which use the natural evolution of a continuous physical system to perform calculations, and more specifically to implement neuromorphic architectures that mimic the biological circuitry of the brain.

The major topics include:

Ø analog neuromorphic computing

Ø memristive computing

Ø optical neural networks

Ø optical spiking networks

Ø diffractive neural networks

Ø Etc.
